HOW TO CREATE AN IMPACTFUL
FREELANCER'S DIGITAL PORTFOLIO

- Shantanu Mishra on 12th May 2022


An online portfolio is an essential tool for promoting your professional work to your potential clients or employers. It is important to present yourself and your work professionally to your hiring manager or clients. So, there are certain tips to redefine your portfolio which would ultimately make your portfolio stand out among others:

1. Keep your portfolio simple and remember your goals.

When someone sees your portfolio the first impression of the viewer should be a good one. So try to design your portfolio to be simple and elegant. Don’t just put jargon as it would make things complicated. Try to design your portfolio in such a way that it would answer basic questions that the client or the hiring manager would ask like: Who is this person? What work they have done before? Where are they based?; Are there any examples of their recent work?; Which other companies have they worked for?; How do I get in touch?. You can also add an intro video that could answer these essential questions.


2. Present only your best work

When it comes to deciding, what to display to your hiring manager or client, remember that they do not need to see everything you have done in your professional life. So, showcase the best work you have done in all of your professional careers. Try to showcase the best projects you have done in different fields of your professional career.

3. Use quality images and display your story

A portfolio will not be able to make a long-lasting impact without having an intriguing image. So, put some images or illustrations related to your project or work to make your portfolio compelling. Next, try to add some detailed descriptions to make and include features of your project.

4. Add a compelling bio

An eye-catching bio that would attract the attention of clients or hiring managers by adding some engaging and compelling things in your bio. Keep your bio jargon-free, be humble and create a confident image of yourself through your bio. Don’t forget to add all your professional experience in your bio, which would reflect your professional achievements or career.

5. Make it easy for the people to contact you

Don’t structure your portfolio in such a way that it will invite some actions from your client and your hiring manager. So provide your contact details which will help be beneficial for you in the long run.
